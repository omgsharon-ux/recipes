Notes

Le Houllier is pronounced "La-who-yer." It is French. Nana Rose has always made this dressing every year for the last 70+ years. This dressing has been handed down from the LeHoullier family for generations. Nana is not sure how old this recipe is, but she remembers her mother, Frances Le Houllier Creely, teaching her to make it. Nana used to grind the giblets raw with her grinder; in this recipe we cook the giblets first then use the food processor — there is no difference in taste. Nana Rose made this for Thanksgiving, Christmas, and Easter, and would also freeze it to serve smaller portions with dinners throughout the year.

This dressing is a pain in the ass to make, but the holidays wouldn't be the same without it.
